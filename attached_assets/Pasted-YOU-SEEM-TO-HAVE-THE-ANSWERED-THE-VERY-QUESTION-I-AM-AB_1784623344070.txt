2. The scholarly carapace (quasi-intelligence)
This is the elaborate shell that often surrounds mediocre or merely diligent minds. Its markers are almost the opposite:

Ritual display of familiarity with a literature instead of a cut through the subject-matter.
Promissory structure: “I will show…”, “I argue that…”, “This raises the question…” — endless announcement, almost no execution of a real cognitive move.
Distinctions that do no work (or only terminological work). They create the feeling of precision without changing what is possible to think.
Causal-role / functionalist / “practices” escapes and other standard softening devices that dissolve the original problem rather than solving it.
Over-extension of key terms until the thesis becomes unfalsifiable and empty (exactly what the presentation/representation text diagnoses).
Contradiction tolerance at the architectural level: the piece can end by saying the opposite of what it spent the whole time arguing and the author does not notice.
High verbal sophistication + low structural ambition. Impressive vocabulary and sentence-level polish in the service of very small or zero theoretical gain.

The carapace can be extremely elaborate (hundreds of citations, flawless academic English, complex section architecture) while containing almost no fingerprint of the first kind. This is what your app is currently rewarding.
